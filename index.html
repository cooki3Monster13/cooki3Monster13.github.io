Tay-Tracks

v0 · viewer

A semantic atlas of Taylor Swift's discography, organized as a tour.

→

Built embeddings over every line of Taylor's catalog, then projected them to 2D so you can wander. Includes an era classifier (88% accuracy on a held-out set of 200 hand-labeled lyrics). I wrote up what the misclassifications taught me about Folklore-vs-Evermore.

The catalog rewards specificity, which is the thing AI generation is worst at. The model surfaces patterns; I do the listening.

under construction Eras Tour viewer demo lives in the repo. The full deployed version — public search, pre-loaded corpus, live classifier — ships in v1.1 (week after launch).

▸ how this evolved
First draft of the era-classifier prompt
apr 28

I started with a one-liner system prompt and a list of 11 eras. The model returned reasonable answers but kept hedging — adding "probably" or explaining its reasoning, which broke my parser.

SYSTEM: Classify the era of this Taylor Swift lyric. Eras: Debut, Fearless, … TTPD.

Output sample: "This is probably Folklore, given the imagery of cardigans and cottages..."

Added era textures + format constraint
apr 29

Two changes: a one-line texture for each era (e.g., "Folklore: indie-folk, fictional characters, cottagecore, melancholy") and an explicit format constraint. The textures were the bigger win — accuracy went from 62% to 79%.

SYSTEM: You are a music classifier… [era + texture × 11]. Respond with ONLY the era name. No explanation.

Misclassifications mostly between Folklore ↔ Evermore. That's actually fair.

Realized my eval set was biased
may 1

Reviewing the misclassifications, I noticed my eval set was 40% Folklore/Evermore (my favorites — duh). Rebuilt it to be stratified ~18 lines per era. Accuracy on the new set: 88%. Honest, calibrated, ready to publish.

eval_set.csv: 198 lines · stratified · 17–19 per era · labeled by hand

Lesson: look at the eval set before trusting the accuracy number.

d3 viewer: hand-coded, not AI'd
may 4

I wrote the d3 visualization myself. Tried asking Claude to scaffold it twice and rolled back both times — the generated code was bloated and got the era-color mapping wrong in subtle ways. Faster to start clean.

Not every part of a project benefits from AI. This is one of them.

Cycle Compass

v0 · designed

RAG over peer-reviewed women's health literature. It refuses when it doesn't know.

→

Women's health info online is a mess. This is a research assistant that only answers from a curated corpus of open-access papers. If retrieval doesn't find a confident source, it refuses to answer and hands back PubMed search terms. The Safety Panel surfaces refusal rate (18%, on purpose) and citation accuracy from a 50-question eval set.

AI is a research assistant here, never the authority. The refusal logic is the whole point — better honest silence than a confident wrong answer about a hormone.

Roadmap, v1.x — opt-in journal-pattern surfacing. Privately-stored journal entries, cross-referenced with cycle phase. When you log a strong feeling, the model can ask "is this how you usually feel in this phase?" — instead of answering for you. Local-first; the corpus never leaves your device. The point isn't to be an oracle. The point is helping you learn to trust your own pattern.

under construction v0 RAG architecture + eval methodology designed. v1 deploys with the public corpus and refusal logic. v1.x adds the opt-in journal-pattern feature once the privacy architecture is locked.

MS Paint 2.0

v0 · demo

Made by hand. Verified by mind. Optional AI second opinion.

→

I grew up on MS Paint. I am suspicious of every AI image generator. This is the painting tool I wish existed in 2026 — pixel-perfect, intentional, slow on purpose. It has no "generate" button.

AI shows up in three deliberate ways and nowhere else: it can describe what you drew in plain text so you can check that the picture matches your intent; it can critique your color balance or value range if asked; and it can find reference photos for you to study — the way an art-school instructor would. AI is the teaching assistant, not the artist.
